Same vats. Same family. Since 1946.

The Gile family has operated the Carr Cheese Factory for four generations. Nothing about the process is rushed: milk comes in, curd gets worked by hand, and the trophy shelf keeps filling up — 200+ awards and a Wisconsin State Champion title.

The outlet store sits at the front of the factory, so the distance from vat to your cooler is measured in steps, not miles.

Vat to bag, one morning

  1. Fresh Wisconsin milk hits the vat before sunrise.
  2. Curd is cut, cooked, and worked by hand.
  3. Cheddar is pressed and aged — up to 5 years.
  4. Curds are bagged the same day. If they don't squeak, they don't sell.
